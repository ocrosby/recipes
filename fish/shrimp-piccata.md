# Shrimp Piccata

A 20-minute weeknight take on chicken piccata. Same lemon-caper-butter sauce, done with shrimp instead — quicker, lighter, and easier to nail because shrimp cook in about a minute per side. Serve over angel hair, or straight from the pan with crusty bread.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb raw shrimp, peeled and deveined (tails on if you like)
- 3 tbsp flour
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter (divided — 1 tbsp for the pan, 1 for finishing)
- 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup dry white wine (or dry vermouth)
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1/2 cup chicken broth
- 2 tbsp capers, drained
- 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, minced
- Angel hair pasta, cooked al dente, for serving (optional)

## Instructions

1. Season the flour with a good pinch of salt and pepper in a shallow dish. Toss the shrimp in the flour until lightly coated — just a dusting, not a heavy crust.
2. Heat the olive oil in a large nonstick or stainless skillet over medium heat. Add the shrimp in a single layer, cook 2–3 minutes on the first side, flip, cook one more minute. Move them to a plate — they'll finish in the sauce later, so slightly underdone here is fine.
3. Melt 1 tbsp of the butter in the same pan. Add the sliced garlic and stir for about 30 seconds — you want it golden, not brown.
4. Pour in the white wine, lemon juice, and chicken broth. Turn the heat up to medium-high and let the sauce reduce for about 5 minutes, until it thickens slightly.
5. Take the pan off the heat. Whisk in the remaining tablespoon of butter until the sauce goes glossy. Add the capers and parsley, then return the shrimp to the pan and turn to coat.
6. Taste, adjust salt and pepper, and serve immediately over pasta.

## Notes

- **Wine substitute.** Dry vermouth is the closest sub if you don't want to open a bottle of wine — same acid, more herbal. In a pinch, use extra broth plus a splash more lemon.
- **Don't overcook the shrimp.** They go from tender to rubbery in about 30 seconds. Pull them from the pan when they're just barely opaque and let the residual heat from the sauce finish them.
- **Scale down.** Halves fine for two people. The sauce reduces faster in less liquid — start checking at 3 minutes.
