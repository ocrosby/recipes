# Curry Lentil Soup

Serves 4.

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons Olive oil
- 1 large Onion, chopped
- 2 Carrots, cut into discs
- 3 cloves Garlic, grated
- 1 teaspoon Ginger, grated
- 2 teaspoons Curry powder
- 1 teaspoon Tumeric powder
- 1 teaspoon Ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon Red pepper flakes (more or less to taste)
- 5 cups Vegetable broth
- 1 can (15 oz) Crushed tomatoes
- 1 cup Dry lentils, green or brown
- 1 pound Potatoes, peeled and cut into bite-size chunks
- 5 oz Kale or spinach, chopped
- 1 teaspoon Salt (or more to taste)
- 1/8 teaspoon Black pepper

## Instructions

- Heat the olive oil in a large pot
- Add the chopped onion and carrot discs and sauté for about 3 minutes
- Stir in the grated garlic, ginger, curry powder, tumeric, ground cumin, and red pepper flakes
- Cook for 1 more minute
- Rinse the lentils and add them to the pot
- Add the vegetable broth
- Add the can of crushed tomatoes
- Add the peeled and cubed potatoes
- Add the chopped kale or spinach
- Season with salt and black pepper
- Simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are tender
- Taste and adjust the seasoning before serving

## Serving Suggestions

Serve with any combination of:

- Boiled brown rice
- A squeeze of fresh lime juice
- A dollop of Greek yogurt
- Pickled red onions
- Chopped scallions

## Substitutions

- Oil: coconut oil or avocado oil instead of olive oil
- Lentils: canned (drained) — reduce the broth and simmer for 15-20 minutes instead of 30
- Potatoes: sweet potatoes or squash
- Greens: spinach, chard, bok choi, or cabbage in place of kale

## Storage

- Refrigerator: up to 4 days in an airtight container
- Freezer: up to 3 months
- Reheating: microwave or stovetop; add a splash of water if it has thickened
