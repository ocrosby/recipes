# French Onion Soup

Serves 4.

## Ingredients

- 4 tablespoons Unsalted butter
- 2 tablespoons Olive oil
- 4 large Yellow onions, halved and thinly sliced (about 2 pounds)
- 1 teaspoon Salt
- 1 teaspoon Sugar
- 2 cloves Garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon All-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup Dry white wine (or dry sherry)
- 6 cups Beef broth
- 3 sprigs Fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried)
- 1 Bay leaf
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1/2 teaspoon Black pepper
- 1 Baguette, cut into 1/2-inch rounds
- 2 cups Gruyère cheese, freshly grated (about 8 oz)

## Instructions

- Melt the butter with the olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat
- Add the sliced onions, salt, and sugar and stir to coat
- Cook uncovered, stirring every few minutes, for 40 to 50 minutes until the onions are deep golden brown and jammy — do not rush this step, caramelization is the whole recipe
- Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute
- Sprinkle the flour over the onions and stir to coat, cooking for another minute to remove the raw taste
- Pour in the wine (or sherry) and scrape the bottom of the pot to lift the browned bits
- Add the beef broth, thyme, bay leaf, and Worcestershire sauce
- Bring to a simmer and cook uncovered for 20 minutes to concentrate the flavor
- Fish out the bay leaf and any thyme stems
- Season with black pepper and taste for salt
- While the soup simmers, toast the baguette slices under the broiler until golden on both sides
- Ladle the soup into oven-safe crocks or bowls set on a sheet pan
- Top each bowl with a layer of toasted baguette slices and a generous mound of grated Gruyère
- Broil 4 to 5 inches from the heat for 2 to 3 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and browned in spots
- Serve immediately — the bowls will be very hot

## Serving Suggestions

Serve with any combination of:

- A simple green salad with sharp vinaigrette
- A glass of the same dry white wine you cooked with
- More toasted baguette on the side for the last of the broth
- Cornichons or pickled onions to cut the richness

## Substitutions

- Onions: Vidalia or sweet onions caramelize faster; red onions add color but less body
- Wine: dry vermouth in place of white wine; brandy or cognac (1 tablespoon) alongside sherry for extra depth
- Broth: half beef, half chicken for a lighter version; homemade stock if you have it
- Cheese: Emmental, Comté, or a Swiss-Gruyère blend if Gruyère isn't available; Provolone in a pinch
- Bread: any sturdy country loaf that will hold up under broiled cheese without turning to mush

## Storage

- Refrigerator: up to 4 days in an airtight container (soup only, no bread or cheese)
- Freezer: up to 3 months
- Reheating: warm the soup on the stovetop, then assemble the bread and cheese and broil fresh — pre-topped bowls do not reheat well

## Notes

- Slice the onions before you start; you'll be at the stove for at least 45 minutes and there's no time to prep once caramelization is going
- If the onions start to burn instead of brown, the heat is too high — lower it and add a splash of water to deglaze
- The best French Onion Soup is patient; the difference between a 25-minute caramelization and a 45-minute one is enormous
