# Beef Stew

Serves 6.

A classic braised beef stew: chuck roast browned in bacon fat, deglazed with red wine, and simmered low with root vegetables until fork-tender.

## Ingredients

### Beef

- 2 1/2 pounds Beef chuck roast, cut into 1 1/2-inch cubes
- 1/4 cup All-purpose flour
- 1 1/2 teaspoons Salt
- 1/2 teaspoon Black pepper
- 4 slices Bacon, chopped
- 2 tablespoons Olive oil (if needed)

### Aromatics

- 1 large White onion, chopped
- 2 stalks Celery, chopped
- 3 cloves Garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons Tomato paste

### Braising liquid

- 1 cup Dry red wine (Cabernet or Merlot)
- 4 cups Beef broth
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 2 Bay leaves
- 1 teaspoon Dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon Dried rosemary

### Vegetables

- 1 pound Yukon gold potatoes, cut into 1-inch chunks
- 4 Carrots, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 2 stalks Celery, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1 cup Pearl onions, peeled (frozen works)
- 1 cup Cremini mushrooms, quartered

### Finish

- 2 tablespoons Fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

## Instructions

### Preheat

- Preheat the oven to 325°F

### Prep the beef

- Pat the beef cubes very dry with paper towels
- In a bowl, toss the beef with flour, salt, and pepper until evenly coated

### Render the bacon

- Heat a large Dutch oven over medium heat
- Add the chopped bacon and cook until crisp and the fat is rendered
- Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the fat in the pot

### Sear the beef

- Working in batches to avoid crowding, sear the beef cubes in the bacon fat over medium-high heat
- Brown on all sides, about 2-3 minutes per side
- Add olive oil between batches if the pot runs dry
- Transfer the seared beef to a plate

### Build the base

- Reduce heat to medium
- Add the chopped onion and celery and cook until softened, about 4 minutes
- Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute
- Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes until it darkens

### Deglaze

- Pour in the red wine, scraping up all the browned bits from the bottom of the pot
- Simmer for 3-4 minutes to reduce slightly

### Braise

- Return the beef and bacon to the pot
- Add the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaves, thyme, and rosemary
- Bring to a simmer
- Cover and transfer to the oven
- Braise for 1 1/2 hours

### Add the vegetables

- Remove the pot from the oven
- Stir in the potatoes, carrots, celery, pearl onions, and mushrooms
- Return to the oven, covered, for another 45 minutes to 1 hour, until the beef and vegetables are fork-tender

### Finish

- Discard the bay leaves
- Taste and adjust salt and pepper
- If the stew is too thin, simmer uncovered on the stovetop for 5-10 minutes to reduce
- Stir in the fresh parsley just before serving

## Serving Suggestions

Serve with any combination of:

- Crusty bread for sopping up the gravy
- Buttered egg noodles
- Mashed potatoes
- A glass of the same red wine used in the braise

## Substitutions

- Beef: brisket or bottom round instead of chuck (chuck is best for tenderness)
- Wine: additional beef broth plus 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
- Potatoes: turnips, parsnips, or rutabaga
- Mushrooms: baby bella or button mushrooms

## Tips

- Pat the beef bone-dry before flouring — a wet surface won't sear, it will steam
- Do not skip the bacon — the rendered fat carries flavor into every layer
- Sear in batches; crowding drops the pan temperature and the beef will gray instead of brown
- The stew tastes better the next day; make it ahead if you can

## Storage

- Refrigerator: up to 4 days in an airtight container
- Freezer: up to 3 months
- Reheating: stovetop over low heat, adding a splash of broth if it has thickened; avoid boiling
