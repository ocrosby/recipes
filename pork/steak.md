# Cast-Iron Pork Shoulder Steaks with Garlic‑Thyme Pan Sauce

A deeply savory, rustic pork dish built for cast‑iron cooking. Pork shoulder steaks deliver rib‑level flavor in a fraction of the time, finished with a simple butter‑basted pan sauce of garlic and thyme.

---

## Why This Recipe Works

* Pork shoulder stays **juicy and flavorful** under high heat
* Cast‑iron provides a **deep, even sear**
* Butter, garlic, and thyme create a **rich but restrained pan sauce**
* Simple technique, restaurant‑quality result

---

## Ingredients

* 2 pork shoulder steaks (¾–1 inch thick)
* Kosher salt, to taste
* Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
* 1 tsp smoked paprika
* 1 tsp garlic powder
* 1 tbsp neutral oil (avocado, canola, or grapeseed)
* 2 tbsp unsalted butter
* 3–4 cloves garlic, smashed
* 3–4 sprigs fresh thyme (or rosemary)
* Optional: splash of chicken stock or dry white wine (for deglazing)

---

## Equipment

* Cast‑iron skillet
* Tongs
* Spoon (for basting)

---

## Instructions

1. **Season the pork**

   * Pat pork shoulder steaks dry.
   * Season generously on both sides with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and garlic powder.

2. **Preheat the skillet**

   * Heat cast‑iron skillet over medium‑high until very hot.
   * Add neutral oil and swirl to coat.

3. **Sear the pork**

   * Place steaks in the skillet and do not move them.
   * Sear 4–5 minutes per side until a deep golden‑brown crust forms.

4. **Build the pan sauce**

   * Reduce heat to medium.
   * Add butter, garlic, and thyme to the pan.
   * Tilt skillet and continuously baste pork with melted butter for 1–2 minutes.

5. **Optional deglaze**

   * Add a small splash of chicken stock or white wine.
   * Scrape up browned bits and spoon sauce over pork.

6. **Rest and serve**

   * Remove pork from skillet and rest for 5 minutes.
   * Spoon remaining pan sauce over the steaks before serving.

---

## Doneness Guide

* Target internal temperature: **145°F (63°C)**
* Pork shoulder can handle slight over‑shoot without drying out

---

## Serving Suggestions

* Crispy roasted potatoes or mashed potatoes
* Green beans, Brussels sprouts, or roasted carrots
* Crusty bread for pan sauce

---

## Variations

* **Herb swap:** Use rosemary or sage instead of thyme
* **Spicy:** Add red pepper flakes or chili crisp at finish
* **Smoky‑sweet:** Finish with a drizzle of honey or maple syrup

---

## Notes

* Pork shoulder steaks are sometimes labeled "pork steaks" or sliced Boston butt
* Letting the meat sit at room temperature for 20 minutes before cooking improves sear
